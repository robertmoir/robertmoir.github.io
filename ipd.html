Iterated Prisoner's Dilemma Tournament

The prisoner's dilemma is a famous simple game where two prisoner's have the choice to either co-operate (C) with their accomplice by not confessing or defect (D) by providing evidence against their accomplice for a lesser penalty. But the outcome depends on the choices of both prisoners.

A famous basic result of game theory says that for both prisoners the dominant strategy, the one with the best average outcome over the possible choices of the opponent, is to defect by ratting on their accomplice, even though if both prisoners co-operate they would both see less jail time than if they both defect.

This game can, however, be iterated, leading to a wide variety of potentially advantageous strategies to be explored. Here a strategy is to specify a sequence of single-game choices, which can depend on the history of choices made by your opponent. It is therefore interesting to host tournaments to allow different choices of strategy to be measured against each other.
