What I do

I have a wide range of interests that include but are not limited to:

cybersecurity

I am now completing the Palette X Fields Accelerated Cybersecurity Program. Here I acquired a diverse background in many cybersecurity topics including computer networkds (infrastructure and security), cryptography and PKI, IAM, PAM, as well as cloud, defensive and offensive security. This included practical experience with router configuration, as well as defensive (firewall, Splunk) and offensive (nmap, Burp Suite, Nessus) tools.

data science

I have a background in SQL and Python tools for data science, including numpy, matplotlib, seaborn, scipy.stats, scikit-learn and keras. With a strong background in advanced mathematics and a capacity for strategic thinking, I bring a powerful set of skills to bear on data science problems.

mathematical software development

I have over 5 years experience in the development of mathematical software, as part of doctoral work in applied mathematics and postdoctoral work in computer science. This has involved the development of algorithms for symbolic integration that enables one to make reliable inferences, and the implementation of highly complex algorithms from computational algebraic geometry to solve nonlinear systems exactly.

scientific epistemology

A long-standing interest of mine is a conceptual understanding of scientific inference in practice and how inferences are made both efficient and reliable. This interest began with my research in philosophy of science, developed in my work in applied mathematics and continues as I explore statistical inference methods in data science and AI.

ethical ai

An emerging interest of mine is the ethical use of AI. This area includes the development of algorithms and toolkits that make AI more trustworthy, transparent and fair, as well as strategies to assist companies in adapting their business practices, to make them both more ethical and more responsive to potential harms. I am currently just exploring this space.
